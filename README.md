## ABC Polyspecificity
Code for the following publication can be found in the [`polyspecificity-abc`](polyspecificity-abc) folder:

**A comprehensive protein structures- and machine learning-based workflow to identify polyspecific binding sites for drug development**

Gergely Gyimesi, Shouyu Wang, Hauke Busch, Sven Marcel Stefan.

> **Motivation:** Drug development suffers from high attrition reflected in high costs and low approval rates. Adverse events are often rooted in off-target effects eventually resulting in termination of drug development programs. Drug disposition is strongly attributed to ATP-binding cassette (ABC) transporters, which are a model protein superfamily for off-target binding given their ability to attract chemically diverse ligands. The identification of structural elements ultimately responsible for off-target binding would benefit current efforts for a more cost- and resource-effective drug development.\
**Results:** We developed a machine learning-(ML-)assisted computational workflow for the prediction of polyspecific binding sites under use of all available vertebrate ABC transporter structures. Characteristic protein–ligand interactions, surface area profiles, and amino acid compositions could be identified distinguishing off-target from on-target binding. High lipophilicity, low polarity, and distinct amino acids (*e.g.*, methionine) mediate key interactions which could be segregated by a support vector machine learning model – ultimately allowing for the prediction of binding site polyspecificity, and thus, drug promiscuity from a structural perspective, improving future structure-based drug design approaches.
